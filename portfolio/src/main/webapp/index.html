My Background

My full name is Mirriam Chemutai Ronoh. I was born and raised in Kericho County, Kenya. A little bit about my names; my grandmother gave me the name Mirriam, it's a Biblical name, though it's spelt Miriam in the Bible. In my tribe (Kipsigis), middle names are given with respect to the time of birth. I was named Chemutai because I was born at dawn. 'Mutai' means dawn in my tribe and 'Che' is a girl's name's prefix. Ronoh is my surname. I went to an all girls' boarding school, Alliance Girls' High School, in a small town called Kikuyu, which is about an hour from the capital, Nairobi. After graduating from high school, I took a gap year before joining college. At the time, I was working and applying for college. I was fortunate to gain an admission to the University of Pennsylvania. At the time, I was still undecided on what I wanted to pursue, and after taking some Computer Science classes, I declared a Computer and Information Science major after my freshman year. I also plan to minor in Mathematics. Looking ahead, I am still quite unsure about what I want to do after graduation. I am interested in pursuing a Masters or a pHD program in Machine Learning. Artficial Intelligece fascinates me and I would love to be part of the commmunity that is trying to positively change various aspects of life through computer technology.

During me free time, I like to stay indoors. Mostly, I like putting on headphones and clean the house, organize my space or cook something I like. On a Friday night, you'd likely find me keeping up with a show on Netflix, going to catch a movie at the theatre or just sleeping. My weekends are mostly spent doing some schoolwork and hanging out with friends. On new interests, I am trying to learn how to play a violin and cook better.

Projects

I haven't really worked on personal projects yet but I have worked on some really interesting projects as part of my class assignments. Below are some of my favorites (all implemented in Java):

(a)Travelling Salesperson Problem: In a problem with thousnads of destinations and distances between any two destinations, finding the shortest distance through all the destinations can be daunting. This problem was implemeted by use of simple iteration techniques such as a while loop and linked lists to maintain the order of traversion of the whole map.

(a)Spellchecker: Built a program that checks and corrects grammar mistakes in a file. This was accomplished by using File I/O to read an input file and every word mispelt corrected by rearranging the letters into dictionary words. The new grammatically-correct file is then made available to the user by writing to an output file.

(a)Pennpals: Created an interactive chatting platform that enables users to register by assigning each user a distinct ID and username. Some users can create groups (channels), each with its name, where one user is the owner who admits other members and can also remove members. Users can send send messages to each other and also to channels.

(a)Hashing and Tries: Implemented a working HashMap using a resizing linked list. Every new item inserted into the map is assigned a location with respect to its key hashcode.
